Wing Tsung

Wing Tsung is the form of Wing Chun Kung Fu developed by Grand Master Dai-Sifu Reimers, which originated in China around 300 years ago. It is designed for maximum effective self-defense.

Strictly adhering to the traditional forms, mottos, and principles of this fascinating martial art, Wing Tsung is designed to defend against all forms of attack in everyday life: long-range combat, close combat, ground fighting, armed opponents, unarmed opponents, “street fighters,” or martial artists (judo, jiu-jitsu, Muay Thai, boxing, karate, etc.).

Due to its relentless effectiveness, Wing Tsung is also trained by special forces such as the FBI, KSK, or Spetsnaz. It therefore forms the basis for fascinating fight scenes in Hollywood productions such as Taken, Bourne Identity, or Ip Man.

But Wing Tsung is more than just self-defense. Wing Tsung is the internalization of the most important teachings of three millennia-old philosophies (Confucianism, Buddhism, Taoism). Therefore, it also imparts inner strength and balance in everyday professional and private life.
